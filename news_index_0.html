Two Papers Presented to SC 2025!

We are excited to announce that two papers from PADSYS Lab have been accepted and presented at SC 2025:

  • "HPC-R1: Characterizing R1-like Large Reasoning Models on HPC"
  • "DPAR: High-Performance, Secure, and Scalable Differential Privacy-based AllReduce"

These works explore emerging directions in large-scale AI systems and secure distributed training.

Congratulations to all authors!

Strong Publication Year at Top Systems Conferences!

PADSYS Lab has had a productive year with multiple papers accepted to top systems venues, including SC 2025, HPDC 2025, ICS 2025, PPoPP 2025, IPDPS 2025, SIGMOD 2025, and SEC 2025.

These works span distributed systems, machine learning, privacy-preserving computing, DPU-enabled computing, federated learning, and data-intensive applications, reflecting our continued efforts in advancing scalable and high-performance computing systems.

Congratulations to all students and collaborators!

Best Paper Award Nomination at IPDPS 2024!

Our paper "Accelerating Lossy and Lossless Compression on Emerging BlueField DPU Architectures" has received a Best Paper Award Nomination at IPDPS 2024.

This work explores efficient compression techniques on emerging DPU architectures for high-performance systems.

Congratulations to Yuke Li and the team!

NSF CAREER Award Received!

Prof. Xiaoyi Lu has received the NSF CAREER Award.

This award supports research on heterogeneity-enriched communication for next-generation HPC and AI systems, as well as education and outreach activities.

We are excited to advance this research direction! Thanks a lot for NSF's support!
